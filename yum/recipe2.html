Lemon Loaf

with lemon glaze

2

loaves

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 c cake flour
  • 1 1/2 c all-purpose flour
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 1/4 c sugar
  • 8 lg eggs at room temp
  • 1/4 c grated lemon zest (4 lemons)
  • 1/4 c fresh lemon juice
  • 2 c unsalted butter, melted and cooled
  • 1/2 c sour cream, at room temperature
  • 2 tsp pure vanilla extract
  • 1/3 c fresh lemon juice
  • 1/3 c sugar

Preheat

oven to 350 F. Spray sides and bottom of two bread pans with nonstick cooking spray and line the bottom with parchment paper, then spray the paper.

Sift

flours, baking powder, baking soda, and salt together in a medium sized bowl.

Combine

sugar, eggs, lemon zest, and lemon juice in a food processor. While mixing, slowly add butter. Add the sour cream and vanilla and pulse until combined. Transfer the mixture to a large bowl.

Fold

in dry mixture in one-third intervals until just combined. Do not overmix.

Divide

batter evenly between prepared pans and bake in the center of the oven for 20 minutes.

Rotate

pans and reduce oven temperature to 325 F, then bake for another 30 to 35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted in the center of the loaf comes out clean. Let cool in the pans for 15 minutes. Meanwhile, make the syrup.

Combine

lemon juice and sugar in a small saucepan over medium heat. Once sugar is dissolved, cook for 3 minutes. Remove from the heat and set aside.

Invert

loaves onto a half sheet pan lined with parchment paper. Use a toothpick to poke holes in the tops and sides of the loaves.

Brush

tops and sides of the loaves with the lemon syrup. Let the syrup soak into the cake and brush again. Let cool completely, at least 30 minutes.

The soaked but unglazed loaves will keep, wrapped in two layers of plastic wrap and frozen, for up to 6 weeks
